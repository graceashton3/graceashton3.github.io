What is Figure Skating?

Figure skating is a sport that combines elements of ice skating, gymnastics, and dance. Skaters perform various jumps, spins, and footwork while skating to music. It is both an individual and a team sport depending on the format.

In figure skating, spinning is a move where a skater rotates on one foot while maintaining balance and control. Spins can vary in position, speed, and difficulty, and are judged based on execution and technique.

Best Times to Skate

Figure skating is practiced all year round, but the best times for competitive events are usually during winter seasons. Most figure skating competitions are held in the colder months, though all practice throughout the year in indoor rinks.

Month Recommended Events
January National Championships
March World Figure Skating Championships
June Summer Skating Camps

This kind of schedule is for the elite figure skaters. If you are looking to skate, find a rink and go, they are open year-round!

Best Locations for Figure Skating

How to Get Started in Figure Skating

If you are interested in figure skating, you want to start by taking beginner lessons at your local ice rink. You can do this by signing up for the learn-to-skate program, where it will take you through the beginning steps of figure skating.

Why I Love Figure Skating

Figure Skating combines athleticism, artistry, and grace. This sport is hard, it challenges you each day, and constantly pushes you to improve.

The feeling when you finally land a jump you have been working really hard on or complete a really good program and medal is a feeling like none other. It makes all the hard and difficult times disappear.
